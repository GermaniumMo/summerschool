History

AI Timeline

The evolution of artificial intelligence from its inception to the present day.

1956

Birth of AI

The Dartmouth Conference marks the official birth of artificial intelligence as a field of study. John McCarthy coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" and brought together pioneers like Marvin Minsky, Allen Newell, and Herbert Simon.

1966

ELIZA chatbot

Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT creates ELIZA, the first chatbot. It simulated conversation using pattern matching, demonstrating early natural language processing capabilities.

1980

Expert Systems

Expert systems become commercially viable, encoding human expertise in narrow domains. MYCIN, developed at Stanford, demonstrated medical diagnosis capabilities.

1997

Deep Blue Victory

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, marking AI's first major milestone in defeating human champions in complex strategic games.

2011

Watson Wins Jeopardy

IBM's Watson defeats champion Ken Jennings on Jeopardy!, showcasing advanced natural language processing and knowledge retrieval capabilities.

2012

Deep Learning Era

AlexNet wins ImageNet competition with unprecedented accuracy, igniting the deep learning revolution in computer vision and establishing neural networks as the dominant AI approach.

2016

AlphaGo Victory

Google DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats world Go champion Lee Sedol, demonstrating AI's ability to master intuitive games beyond computational brute force.

2020

GPT-3 Released

OpenAI releases GPT-3 with 175 billion parameters, showcasing unprecedented language generation and reasoning capabilities that sparked the LLM revolution.

2022

Diffusion Models

Stable Diffusion and DALL-E 2 popularize generative AI for images, enabling text-to-image creation and democratizing AI creativity for mainstream users.

2024

Multimodal AI

AI systems now seamlessly process text, images, audio, and video simultaneously. GPT-4V, Gemini, and Claude demonstrate multimodal reasoning capabilities.
